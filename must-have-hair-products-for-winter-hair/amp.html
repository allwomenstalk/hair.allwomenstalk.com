7 Must-Have Hair Products for Winter Hair ...

Alterna Smooth anti-Breakage Thermal Protectant Spray • Garnier 3 Minute Undo Dryness Reversal Treatment • Batiste Dry Shampoo • L’Oreal Professionnel Mythic Oil • Kiehl’s Olive Fruit Oil Deeply Reparative Hair Pak • More ...

By Sophia

Hair Products can have a range of benefits when it comes to protecting your hair from seasonal elements. In winter, hair tends to be subjected to dryness and exposure to a range of different damaging elements (think hairdryers and straightening irons). It’s important then to invest in hair products with moisturizing and nourishing properties. Take a look at a few hair products worth investing in this winter.

1. Alterna Smooth anti-Breakage Thermal Protectant Spray

Price: $24.00 at sephora.com
A heat protecting spray is a wise investment, especially if you’ll be exposing your hair to hair dryers and straightening irons on a regular basis this winter. This product offers thermal protection up to 428 degrees and provides anti-breakage protection. And this heat protecting spray right here is made from certified organic ingredients, as well as being eco-certified and fair-trade compliant.

2. Garnier 3 Minute Undo Dryness Reversal Treatment

Price: $6.29 at drugstore.com
Reverse the negative effects caused by the winter weather with hair products known as conditioning treatments! Why? Because a deep conditioning treatment done once a week revitalizes hair leaving it soft, supple and shiny . This product right here is formulated with olive, avocado, and shea oils which are known for their amazing abilities to nourish and replenish hair. Use this product instead of your normal conditioner, and leave in for three minutes before rinsing out.

3. Batiste Dry Shampoo

Price: $3.99 at drugstore.com
A dry shampoo is a perfect way to touch up hair between washes and make sure that it never looks dirty or bad in any way. This all time favorite works by absorbing excess oil instantly making your hair look fresher thus allowing to avoid speedy but damaging blow-drying. So, if you don't have the time to let your hair dry naturally now, simply reschedule your wash for the evening and rely on a dry shampoo to help you have a gorgeous hairstyle despite the fact that your hair hasn't been conventionally washed.

4. L’Oreal Professionnel Mythic Oil

Price: $19.74 at amazon.com
Fight winter fizz with this nourishing oil. This product is made with a blend of avocado and grape seed oil and it offers anti-frizz protection and gives hair a light, glossy finish. At around $20, it’s on the pricier end of the hair products scale but if you’re after something that works wonders then this is it.

5. Kiehl’s Olive Fruit Oil Deeply Reparative Hair Pak

Price: $25.00 at shop.nordstrom.com
If your hair could be best described as dry and damaged, then this reparative mask is worth trying out. The conditioning mask I've decided to suggest today is actually designed with chemically-processed and heat-styled hair in mind and it helps to restore hair’s natural moisture content. What could be better than that!

6. John Frieda Flyaway Tamer

Price: $7.99 at drugstore.com
Purse-sized hair products such as this one are an absolutely perfect way to ensure optimal hair care on the go. Formulated with Inca Inchi Oil, this product is designed to tame flyaways and frizz while its innovative, mascara-like shape ensures effortless, piece-of-cake simple application -simply brush the wand over affected areas and reapply when needed!

7. Phyto Phytheol Intense anti-Dandruff Treatment Shampoo

Price: $24.00 at sephora.com
Dry weather also means dry skin and dry skin on your scalp often means dandruff. Uh-oh - if there could be a way to prevent this! Well, keep your scalp dandruff-free this winter with the help of this specially formulated shampoo which won't only relieve the symptoms but also prevent the reappearance of dandruff. Now, I know this product is a bit pricier but fear not - there is a wide range of cheaper hair products available out there as well!

Now, I’m not saying you should go out and buy all of these hair products, but these are just a few that target typical winter-related hair problems. And, a little tip on the side- When looking for hair products this winter, stick to the ones that offer extra nourishing or protective properties.

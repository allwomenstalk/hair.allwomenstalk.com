# 7 Ways to Figure out What Haircut You Want ...

To figure out what haircut you want, you have to do some thinking. You don't want to show up at the salon and tell the stylist to surprise you with a new look. You have to make sure that you're comfortable with whatever decision you make, because your hair is one of the first things that others will notice about you. Here are a few helpful ways to figure out what haircut you want:

## 1. Look All around

Is there a celebrity or friend that you're always jealous of, because their hair looks stunning? Could you picture yourself wearing your hair in the same way? One of the easiest ways to figure out what haircut you want is by looking around you. Watch strangers walk by and flip through magazines. You're bound to find something you like, so make sure to keep your eyes open.

## 2. Save It for the Salon

It's scary to go to the salon without any idea of what you want them to do. However, your stylist cuts hair for a living. She should know what kind of cut would flatter your face, and what cut to avoid. You shouldn't let her do whatever she wants without consulting you, but she can throw out a few ideas.

## 3. Figure out Your Face Shape

Figure out what your face shape is, and then type it into your good friend Google. You'll see plenty of images of women who look similar to you, and you can decide what hairstyle you think will flatter you the best. Never forget how useful the Internet can be.

## 4. Check out Websites for Women

There are plenty of different websites that allow you to upload a picture of yourself and "try on" different hairstyles. [Goodhousekeeping.com](https://www.goodhousekeeping.com/beauty/makeup/) lets you try on different types of make-up in addition to trying out different hairstyles. It's a helpful tool for anyone that's worried about how her hair will come out.

## 5. Think It through

Do you really want to cut your hair, or are you just in the mood for a change? There are plenty of ways to switch up your look that don't involve scissors. You can dye your hair, part it differently, or do something completely different, like buy a new wardrobe.

## 6. Give It Time

Don't cut your hair without really thinking about whether you'll be happy with it or not. Even though it'll grow back, you'll have to live with your decision for a few months. Make sure that you take a few days, or even weeks, to think about whether or not you want to go through with the haircut.

## 7. Ask around

Your family and friends may not be professionals, but they see you every day and know what kind of style you can pull off. Before you get your hair chopped off, ask them what they think about the idea you have. In the end, you should do whatever will make you happy, but it can't hurt to hear a few opinions.

Changing your hair can give you an entirely new look. Depending on what you decide to do, you might even end up looking unrecognizable. No matter what the result is, it's a great way to feel fresh and fashionable. What's your current hairstyle? Are you thinking about changing it?

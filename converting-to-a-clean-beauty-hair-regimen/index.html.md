# Converting to a Clean Beauty Hair Regimen ...

Women all over the world are making the transition to clean and natural beauty products. Clean hair products can be described as those that are both safe and non-toxic for people and the environment. When converting to a clean beauty hair regimen, many women often note similarities to the process of making clean eating selections (avoiding processed foods, steering clear of ingredients that sound like components of a science experiment, focusing on whole foods and ingredients that you understand, etc.)

## 1. What It Means to Be Clean

When searching for clean products, the process is quite similar. It is important to avoid processed products that have an endless list of unfamiliar ingredients. Identify whole and natural ingredients that nourish your hair, providing the necessary vitamins and minerals needed for clean and healthy hair. Below, you will find the tools and education necessary to make clean, informed hair care decisions now and in the future.

## 2. Ingredients to Love

In a world that continues to become more and more respectful of clean living, there are many options for [clean hair styling products](https://hairstory.com/shop/category/styling-products/) for women. From local drugstores to upscale specialty shops, women have endless options for beauty products that are both accessible and affordable.

There are many buzzwords that companies use to notify you that their product contains appealing ingredients. Some of these words to look for are clean, green, organic, non-toxic, sustainable, vegan, [cruelty-free](https://beauty.allwomenstalk.com/cruelty-free-beauty-brands-to-shop/), etc. While these words should encourage you to pick up the product, you must still complete your own research by following up with the list of ingredients on the back of the bottle. Here are just a few ingredients to look for when purchasing clean hair care products.

- Aloe Vera
- Avocado and Avocado Oil
- Castor Seed Oil
- Coconut Oil
- Extra Virgin Olive Oil
- Honey
- Jojoba Extract
- Shea Butter
- Tea Tree Oil
- Vegetable Glycerin

## 3. Ingredients to Leave

Sometimes understanding the contents found on the back of hair care products can be quite challenging. There are no warning labels on products with the most toxic ingredients, nor are there gold standards identified as the best clean hair products.

Clean beauty products land on a spectrum of quality. Some should be avoided. Others offer a few clean ingredients. Some include superior clean standards. For this reason, your clean beauty education continues to be very important in your selection process. Beauty experts offer guidance and education by sharing ingredients to avoid. Here are just a few notably [problematic ingredients](https://www.businessinsider.com/dangerous-chemicals-in-beauty-products-makeup-list-2019-6) to steer clear of while perusing the beauty isles.

- Aluminum Compounds
- Ethoxylated Agents
- Formaldehyde
- Fragrances and Phthalates
- Hydroquinone
- Oxybenzone
- Parabens
- Refined Petroleum
- Silica
- Talc
- Triclosan

## 4. Clean Decision Making

Because the hair care beauty industry is not regulated by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA), it is considered to be self-regulated. This means that the power to make clean hair care decisions lies in your hands. When converting to a clean beauty hair regimen, educate yourself on what it means to be clean. Learn about the ingredients to seek on a product label, as well as those to avoid. When armed with the education to make informed decisions, you will find products that embody the natural qualities necessary for a clean beauty hair regimen.

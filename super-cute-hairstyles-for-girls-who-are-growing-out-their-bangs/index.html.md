# 17 Cute Hairstyles for Growing Out Bangs You'll Actually Love

Bangs are undeniably chic, but anyone who's had them knows they can be a bit of a commitment. Once you make the chop, the transition back to long locks often involves an awkward in-between phase that feels impossible to style. If you're currently in the middle of growing out your bangs, don't reach for the scissors just yet! Whether you're dealing with [unruly bangs](https://hair.allwomenstalk.com/how-to-tame-unruly-bangs/) or just looking for fresh inspiration, I've got you covered with styles that are both practical and beautiful.

## 1. Whispy and Shaggy

Own it! If your bangs are reaching that shaggy, lash-grazing length, embrace the rock-and-roll vibe. This effortless look works perfectly with natural texture and gives off a cool, lived-in aesthetic.

## 2. Braided up Perfectly!

One of the best things about bangs is how easily they can be tucked away into a [stunning braid](https://hair.allwomenstalk.com/stunning-braided-hairstyles-for-any-occasion/). This is my personal go-to when I want my hair completely out of my face for a clean, polished look.

## 3. Rope Braids Are the Best

If you're tired of standard three-strand braids, a rope braid is a fantastic alternative. It’s particularly effective for catching those shorter pieces and keeping them secure throughout the day.

## 4. Parted down the Middle

You don't always have to wear your bangs straight forward. As they get longer, try parting them right down the middle for a soft, face-framing effect that blends seamlessly with the rest of your hair.

## 5. Headband It up!

Headbands are a lifesaver during the grow-out phase. They instantly push back any annoying lengths and can add a pop of style or color to your overall look. Getting creative with placement can change your whole vibe.

## 6. Pulling It Back

For an effortless and chic hairstyle, try pulling your bangs back and securing them with a small clip or tucking them behind your ears. It's a simple way to keep your look clean without much fuss.

## 7. Beachy Waves

Beachy waves remain a top-tier hairstyle for a reason—they're incredibly flattering and great for blending different hair lengths. The added texture helps hide any unevenness in your growing bangs.

## 8. Cute Bang Braid

This style is a favorite for dealing with shorter "baby bangs." A tight braid along the hairline is both functional and adorable, keeping every stray hair perfectly in place.

## 9. Throw on a Hat

If you're having a particularly difficult hair day and aren't in the mood for braiding, just throw on a hat! It's the ultimate quick fix that looks intentional and stylish while hiding the struggle.

## 10. Long Wisps in Front

As your bangs reach a more manageable length, you can start leaving just a few soft wisps in the front. This creates a romantic, delicate look that feels intentional rather than like an in-between phase.

## 11. Side-swept It up

Sweep those shorter pieces to the side for an instant change in silhouette. It’s a classic way to manage bangs that are just a little too long for their original cut.

## 12. Parted to the Side

Alternatively, a deep side part can completely transform your look and help tuck those bangs into the rest of your hair. This sophisticated style is perfect for more formal occasions.

Don't let your bangs get in your way at all; instead, slick them back with a bit of gel or pomade for a sleek, modern finish. Check out official products at [Ulta Beauty](https://www.ulta.com) for the best styling hold.

## 14. Held by a Bobby Pin

![Held by a Bobby Pin](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/xu/h9/tgvsjg56574e5ab05278b264395480_480x705.jpg)

Bobby pins are your best friends when you're growing out bangs. Use them to create interesting patterns or simply to pin hair back securely. For high-quality hair accessories, visit [Sephora](https://www.sephora.com).

## 15. Back and in a Headband

![Back and in a Headband](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ap/y3/d9uwq4rx574e5ab0e7eb1547917002_600x900.jpg)

Combining a headband with pinned-back hair is a double-win for security and style. It's easily one of the most comfortable ways to get through the day.

## 16. Fishtail and Bobby

![Fishtail and Bobby](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/r4/ps/je143qbx574e5ab1a3c59140626201_570x776.jpg)

Even if your bangs have reached a significant length, you can still incorporate them into a [fishtail braid](https://hair.allwomenstalk.com/how-to-do-a-fishtail-braid/) for an intricate and beautiful look.

## 17. Two Braids

![Two Braids](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/et/ay/tp9ios91574e5ab570693550417563_600x906.jpg)

Double braids are not only practical for keeping hair secure but also incredibly trendy right now. They're a playful way to manage your transition phase.

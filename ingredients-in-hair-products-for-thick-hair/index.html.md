# 7 Ingredients in Hair Products for Thick Hair ...

If you are looking to have thicker hair, there are certain ingredients to look for in hair products for thick hair. As someone who has fine hair, I am always interested in what ingredients may help my hair look or even grow thicker. Thick hair is so beautiful, but I can’t fight with genetics. I can however give my fine a hair a boost by looking for certain ingredients in hair products for thick hair. If you want thicker hair, read the ingredient label of your hair products for thick hair for some of these ingredients, which can help give you thicker hair.

## 1. Coffee

One surprising ingredient to look for in hair products for thick hair is coffee. You may not think of coffee as doing anything more than giving you a jolt in the morning, but there has been a lot of research done about coffee for hair. Researchers have found that coffee can stimulate hair follicles, which results in hair growth and thicker hair. One study found that coffee increased follicle growth by 46 percent! To get the hair benefits of coffee, you can’t drink it; you have to apply it topically.

## 2. Neem

Neem has long been used to treat hair loss in Southeast Asia. While it treats hair loss, neem can also help thicken hair. Sometimes, thin hair can be a result of excess sebum, which can block hair growth and damage hair follicles. Neem removes excess sebum, and it stimulates circulation, which brings nutrients to the hair roots. All of these benefits help your hair grow as thick as possible.

## 3. Biotin

Biotin supplements are some of the most popular supplements suggested for thicker hair. While you can take supplements, biotin is an ingredient that is often found in hair products. Biotin deficiencies can cause thin hair, and taking a supplement and using products with biotin in them can help thicken your hair.

## 4. Pantothenic Acid

Pantothenic acid is also known as vitamin B5, and it is an important vitamin for hair health. A deficiency in vitamin B5 can result in damaged hair follicles, which will keep you from having thick hair. Like biotin, pantothenic acid is found in both supplements and hair products. Using products with pantothenic acid can help you have thicker hair.

## 5. MSM

MSM stands for methylsulfonylmethane, and it is a naturally occurring sulfur compound. The sulfur in MSM is a necessary building block for hair, because the keratin your hair is composed of requires sulfur. Using MSM products can help your hair grow thicker by giving you the building blocks it needs to be thick and healthy. You can find various hair products with MSM, you just have to look around.

## 6. Saw Palmetto

Saw palmetto is a palm plant that grows in Florida and Georgia. A few studies have been done on this plant, and some have shown that it can promote hair growth. It is particularly beneficial for those who have thinner hair as a result of hormone imbalances. Using products with saw palmetto can help reverse the thinness caused by hormone irregularities and give you thicker hair.

## 7. Essential Fatty Acids

Omega 3 and Omega 6 fatty acids are considered essential. In addition to the health benefits they have, they can also help your hair grow thick by helping with circulation and cell growth. These essential fatty acids (EFAs) also increase nutrient absorption at the hair follicles, which will give you healthier hair. There are many different hair products that contain EFAs, just search the aisles and you will find some.

Thick hair is something everyone wants. While genetics didn’t bless everyone with thick hair, there are ingredients in hair products that can help hair look and grow thicker. Which of these ingredients are you going to be looking for in your hair products?

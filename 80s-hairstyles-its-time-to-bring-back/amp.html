1 The Side High Tail

If you're sick of regular ponytails, make yours on the side.

***

The side high tail is a popular hairstyle from the 80s that is making a comeback in modern times. This hairstyle involves creating a ponytail on the side of the head, rather than in the traditional back position. It adds a unique and edgy touch to any look and is perfect for those who want to switch up their regular ponytail style. It also works well for all hair types and lengths, making it a versatile choice for anyone looking to try a new hairstyle. So if you're looking to add some 80s flair to your hair, give the side high tail a try!

2 The Whale Spout

This can give your hair the volume you've been dreaming about.

***

The Whale Spout hairstyle was a popular 80s style, characterized by big, voluminous hair. The look was achieved by teasing and backcombing the hair at the crown of the head, and then pulling it back into a high ponytail or bun. The look was popularized by celebrities such as Madonna, Cyndi Lauper, and Brooke Shields. Today, the Whale Spout is making a comeback, as more women are looking for a way to add volume to their hair. The style is a great way to switch up your look, and can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. With the right tools and products, you can give your hair the volume you've been dreaming about.

3 Half up

This style is pretty similar to what we currently wear, so you might as well try it.

***

Sure, the half-up hairstyle screams vintage glam with a modern twist. It strikes the perfect balance between casual and chic. To achieve that iconic '80s flair, tease the hair at the crown for some major volume before securing the top half in place. Don't hold back on the hairspray – the '80s were all about maximum hold. For an extra touch of nostalgia, add a scrunchie or opt for bold, chunky accessories. Whether you're heading to the office or hitting the dance floor, this look is versatile, fun, and effortlessly cool.

4 The Wrap up

Don't you have a bandana lying around that you've been waiting to use?

***

Absolutely! That colorful bandana isn't just a nostalgic nod to the 80s—it's your ticket to effortless cool. Fold it into a headband, wrap it around your forehead, or tie it up to accent a high ponytail. The bandana trick is a fab way to infuse some rock 'n' roll vibes into your look while keeping those flyaways tamed. Remember, the bolder the pattern, the bigger the statement, perfect for channeling that Madonna or Pat Benatar energy. So go ahead, dig out that bandana, and rock that retro flair with confidence! 🌟

5 Feathered

There's no reason to keep this hairstyle in the past when it's so fashionable.

***

The Feathered hairstyle is a classic look from the 1980s that has stood the test of time. It is characterized by sweeping layers of hair that frame the face and give it a soft, romantic look. This style works for all hair types and lengths, and can be adapted to fit any face shape. It is also easy to maintain and can be worn both casually and formally. The Feathered hairstyle is a great way to bring a modern twist to a classic look, and is sure to turn heads wherever you go. With a few simple styling techniques, you can easily recreate this look and make it your own.

6 Pulled Back with Ribbon Barrettes

This will make you look super sweet and innocent.

***

Pulled Back with Ribbon Barrettes is a classic 80s hairstyle that is making a comeback. It is a simple, romantic look that is perfect for any occasion. This style involves gathering the hair into a low ponytail and then securing it with ribbon barrettes. The barrettes can be plain or decorated with beads or other embellishments. This look can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion.

The style is easy to achieve and requires minimal effort. First, brush your hair into a low ponytail and secure it with an elastic. Then, take two ribbon barrettes and attach them to the ponytail. Make sure the barrettes are secure and not too loose. You can also add a few bobby pins to the sides of the ponytail to keep the barrettes in place.

To make the look more modern, you can add a few small braids to the ponytail. This will give the style more texture and make it stand out. You can also add a few curls to the ends of the ponytail for an extra touch of glamour.

7 Curls

Stop straightening your hair, because you can't go wrong with curls.

***

Curls are a classic 80s hairstyle that will never go out of style. They are perfect for any occasion and can be styled in a variety of ways. From tight spirals to loose waves, curls can add texture and volume to your hair, creating a look that is both stylish and fun. Curls can also be used to frame the face, creating a softer, more feminine look. With the right products and styling tools, you can create a beautiful, voluminous curl that will last all day. Whether you're looking for a classic 80s style or something more modern, curls are the perfect way to add interest to any look.

8 The Super High Pony

Who doesn't love the ponytail that shows off all your length?!

***

The Super High Pony is a hairstyle that is making a comeback in the fashion world. It's a classic look that is perfect for any occasion, whether you're going for a night out or just to the office. The Super High Pony is a great way to show off your long locks and make a statement.

To achieve the Super High Pony look, start by brushing your hair and create a high ponytail at the crown of your head. Secure the ponytail with an elastic band and then tease the ponytail for extra volume. To finish, wrap a strand of hair around the elastic band and secure with a bobby pin.

The Super High Pony is also a great way to add a bit of glamour to your look. You can accessorize the look with a headband, a bow, or even a sparkly barrette. You can also add a bit of texture to the ponytail by using a curling iron or a flat iron.

9 French Braid

It's cute, fun, and functional! Can't argue with that.

10 Crimping

It's like beach waves only better.

***

Crimping is that rad Zigzag pattern that gives your locks an edgy and voluminous texture - a totally tubular look that's perfect for channeling your inner '80s rockstar goddess. This bodacious style is all about rocking crinkled strands with reckless abandon. To recreate this, grab your crimper and clamp down on small sections of your mane to get that righteous definition. Rock it with a scrunchie or a neon headband, and you'll be ready to hit the roller disco or just slay on your day-to-day. Remember, the bolder, the better. Time to tease it up and let your hair do the talking! 🌟

11 Mega Volume

As if a pouf wasn't enough, just go ahead and tease your whole head or blow dry upside down for some insane volume.

***

Mega volume is a classic 80s hairstyle that is making a comeback. This look is achieved by teasing the hair or blow drying it upside down to create a dramatic volume. This look can be achieved with any hair length or texture and can be tailored to fit any occasion. To get the perfect mega volume look, start by applying a volumizing mousse to damp hair. Brush your hair up from the roots in sections and use a round brush to create more lift. Finish with a strong hold hairspray to lock in the look. For a more extreme volume, try backcombing the hair and use a teasing brush. With the right products and styling techniques, you can achieve the perfect 80s inspired mega volume look.

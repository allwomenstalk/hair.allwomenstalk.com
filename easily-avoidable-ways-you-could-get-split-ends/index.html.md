# 7 Easily Avoidable Ways You Could Get Split Ends ...

No matter what you see on television, split ends are not something you can repair. Certain conditioners and products may allow you to temporary cover your split ends, but they are still there and will show again after the product wears off. What you need to figure out, instead, is how do you get split ends in the first place and focus on changes you'll need to make in order to ensure gorgeous split end-free hair. And reading these 7 points that explain how do you get split ends is a perfect start.

## 1. Diet

You’ve heard it before, proper diet and drinking enough water is not only good for your body, but also great for your hair. Eating good food makes your hair strong and strong hair doesn’t split at the ends. If you are wondering how do you get split ends, it might be time to look at your diet and make a few adjustments.

## 2. More Conditioning

Your hair needs moisture, lots and lots of moisture! So, in order to keep it well hydrated you must use deep conditioners and hydrating hair masques on a regular basis! These products are fairly simple to use at home and extremely easy to find in a local beauty supply shop which means you'll have no problem incorporating them in your beauty routine.

## 3. Less Shampoo

Shampoo may be great to get your hair clean, but it tends to strip your hair of essential oils which protect the ends from splitting. If you shampoo your hair daily then a huge mystery of how do you get split ends isn't really a mystery at all! Yes, your frequent washing is causing your hair to split and the only way to make it better is to try to let it rest a bit between washes. Try washing every other day or every two days - it might be hard at first but your hair and, most importantly, your scalp will get used to it soon and will stop producing excessive sebum that was the reason of your frequent shampooing anyways.

## 4. Heat

If you're wondering how do you get split ends, heat might and probably is one of the right answers. If you constantly use a hair straightener, blow dryer, or curling iron you are causing a serious damage which results in dry hair and, of course, split ends. Restrict the amount of heat used to style your hair ASAP Air dry when you can and opt for leave in rollers every now and then.

## 5. Swimming

Do you like to enjoy laying by the pool and taking a nice dip in the warm summer months? As much as we all enjoy these summer activities, they are also another answer to the question of “how do you get split ends?” The sun, salty water, and chlorine all tend to dry out your hair leaving it damaged and causing the ends to split. Wear a hat when possible, swim with a swimming cap, or consider products that protect the hair from chlorine and salty water.

## 6. Towel Dry

Now - we all know that, although extremely helpful, styling gadgets should be avoided as much as possible. But did you know that you can cause split ends by simply being too rough while towel-drying your hair? Wet hair is more likely to break than dry hair so, instead of wrestling your hair like a mad woman, opt for softer, gentler towel movements . Avoid wringing, twisting, and mashing your hair while it's wrapped inside a towel as these also cause serious damage.

## 7. Trim

The final answer to, “How do you get splint ends,” comes from irregular trims or, even worse the lack of any. You may not need to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks, but you still need to get it trimmed at least every 3 to 4 months to avoid having ragged-looking ends. Frequent trims might not have much to do with speedy hair growth but they will sure help you have fabulous, healthy looking hair and reduce the need for any drastic changes due to drastic, unsightly damages.

And just how do you get split ends? Come on now, after reading all of these popular and quite possible scenarios that explain how do you get split ends, you just can't afford to take the I-don't-know for an answer.

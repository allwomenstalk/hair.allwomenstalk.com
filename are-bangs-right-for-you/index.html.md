# The Secrets to Finding out if Bangs Are for You 🤔 ...

**Bangs** can be a game-changer for your look, trust me-I learned the hard way back in my freshman year of college. I had this grand epiphany that chopping off half my hair into a feathery fringe would make me look like a French New Wave film star. Spoiler alert: it did not. But hey, it’s 2024, and I’ve got insights to share! So, if you’ve ever **mused** while staring at your _reflection_ whether to get bangs, you’re in the right place.

First off, deciding whether **bangs** are for you isn't just a snap decision, folks. It involves a _symphony_ of factors like your face shape, hair type, and yes, even your lifestyle. We've got the time-honored "eyebrow test," the “grab some scissors and hope for the best” method (highly not recommended), and the mystical advice of hair gurus who'll tell you everything from your bang-to-face ratio to the cosmic alignment of your hair parting.

[Face Shapes and Bang Styles: A Perfect Match or an Epic Fail?](#1) Factors like whether you’ve got a heart-shaped, oval, square, or round face can make all the difference in how those fringes frame your _beautiful_ mug. For example, blunt bangs can look stunning on an oval face but might emphasize angles on a square face-something I learned from my _beloved_ cousin who now rocks side-swept bangs like a champ.

And let’s not even get started on **hair texture**. From pin-straight manes to curly crowns, the texture of your hair can turn those bangs from a sleek medal of sophistication into a _frizzy_ tangle of regret.[The High Maintenance Reality of Bangs](#2) tackles this. Trust me, bangs might seem like a low-effort way to change your look, but there's a reason why so many fringes end up pinned back within a week. They might require more styling tools than you can throw a brush at. Let’s just say my hair dryer became my best friend and my enemy at the same time.

Oh, and shout out to all the lazy _weekend warriors_ putting forth minimal effort-pray you never oversleep on work days and forget to tame the wild beast that is your fringe. The struggle is real, folks.

Looking for hacks that’ll make this hairstyle your own without spending hours every day? You won’t want to miss [Quick Tips to Style Your Bangs Like a Pro](#3). These nuggets of wisdom can save you time and potentially keep you from a hair crisis.

So, whether you're a fellow millennial stepping gingerly into the _choppy_ waters of personal style transformations, or a Gen Z daredevil ready to embrace the trend whimsically, buckle up-this is going to be an enlightening voyage into the world of bangs! 🌟

## 1. Try Clip in Bangs

![hair, black hair, person, photography, beauty,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/gr/p6/sz1wl8275831b693356a1190541251_500x500.jpg)

Clip in bangs are a great way to test out bangs if you want to live with the look for a while before committing yourself to the real thing. They're also ideal if you like to swap from one look to the other; you can have bangs one day and go without the day after. If you're going to wear them a lot, be sure to pick a shade that closely matches your own.

## 2. Fake Them with Your Hair

![hair, black hair, face, clothing, person,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/j1/th/wd1hp1mi5831b6dd4fb1e220469898_500x500.jpg)

If your hair is long enough you can also fake bangs with your own hair. You'll need some clips to fix your fake bangs in place. Hold your hair across your forehead until it reaches the length you'd like your bangs, then clip. This should give you an idea of how you'd look with bangs cut in.

## 3. Have a Virtual Makeover

_ With a virtual makeover tool you can try out every different type of bangs with no commitment. Simply take a photo of yourself with your hair tied back and upload it to a site like instyle.com_. You'll easily be able to see whether bangs look awful on you, or if side bangs look great while straight bangs really aren't you.

## 4. Your Face Shape

![hair, human hair color, black hair, face, clothing,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/p1/a3/hlchlgiw5831b72cbe667296583923_500x521.jpg)

The shape of your face can also indicate whether bangs could suit you. If you have a round face, sorry - they don't generally look too flattering on round, full faces. You should also avoid them if you suffer breakouts on your forehead; the oil from your hair will be bad news for your skin.

## 5. What Looks Good on a Celebrity May Not Look Good on You

![hair, human hair color, face, clothing, blond,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/qe/we/i1q9xcs95831b73cd96ae149354167_500x624.jpg)

Yep, we've all gone into a hair salon brandishing a picture of a celebrity and expecting to walk out of there looking every bit as good. But what looks gorgeous on a celeb may not be the right cut for you. So if you want bangs because they look so adorable on Zooey Deschanel or Taylor Swift, check that they work with your face and hair type before going for the chop.

## 6. Maintenance

![hair, black hair, face, clothing, hairstyle,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/kp/bo/e5k0szb95831b74e50aa5949417512_500x495.jpg)

Don't forget that bangs do require maintenance to keep them in shape. You'll probably need to get them trimmed in between cuts to keep them looking good. If you're brave you can trim them yourself (I used to trim mine), but do get a decent pair of hairdressing scissors and only cut if you have a steady hand!

## 7. If You Have Any Doubts …

![hair, black hair, face, person, nose,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ri/lp/t4vlcyw25831b75d5f091357933581_500x571.jpg)

… then don't have bangs cut. Just don't. If you hate them it'll take a long time to grow them out - and growing out bangs is one of the biggest hair pains there is. It drives you mad! Try the clip-in option instead, or go for a side parting that you can pin in place
and give the illusion of bangs.

Bangs can look fantastic, but given that you can't get rid of them quickly you need to be sure that they're right for you. So take your time to decide; never go for the chop on a whim!

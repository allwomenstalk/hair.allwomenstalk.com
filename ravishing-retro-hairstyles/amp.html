1 Loose Retro Waves

Via The Next Best Thing To ...

Loose retro waves create one of the most iconic retro hairstyles you'll ever see. This is a perfect look for medium length hair, especially if it's at the in-between stage.

***

This look is a classic, and is easy to achieve with the right tools. Start by using a curling iron to create loose waves throughout your hair. Then, use a light hairspray to help hold the shape. If you have bangs, you can also use a flat iron to make them look smooth and sleek. To finish, add a bit of shine serum to make your hair look glossy and polished. With a few simple steps, you can create a timeless retro hairstyle that will turn heads.

5 Long Lush Victory Rolls

Via 8 Retro Hairstyles to Try ...

You're about to see a lot of victory rolls, which were particularly popular in the late 1930s and 1940s. Everything about this look is ravishingly retro – I've got some serious style envy for this beauty!

***

Long Lush Victory Rolls is a retro hairstyle that was particularly popular in the late 1930s and 1940s. It is characterized by its voluminous curls and intricate styling. The look is created by using a curling iron or curlers to create large, loose curls, and then pinning them into an updo. Victory rolls can be styled with a single roll on the top of the head, or multiple rolls around the face.

The look was popularized by Hollywood stars such as Rita Hayworth, Betty Grable, and Veronica Lake. It was often seen in classic movies and was a staple of the pin-up girl look. The style was also popular among women in the military during World War II.

Today, Long Lush Victory Rolls is still a popular look. It is often seen on the red carpet and is a great way to add a vintage flair to any look. The style is also popular among brides who want to add a classic touch to their wedding day look.
